# CHAPTER 02 — The Four Images

### *Sìxiàng* — the four directions · the number `4`

> *Each breath has two ends, and each end has two moods. Out can be a sigh or a shout; in can be a
> gasp or a rest. So the Two were really Four all along.*

---

## 🧭 Cold open — the light won't go one way home

The color climbing out of the Tide Pools doesn't flow uphill in a single stream. At the top of the
beach path it **forks** — and forks again — and races off toward four different corners of the grove
at once, like it knows exactly where it's going and you don't.

Mimi runs after it, then stops at a place you hadn't noticed before: a mossy crossroads with **four
old stone altars**, one at each compass point, each dark, each carved with a different mark.

> **Mimi:** *(turning slowly, reading them)* "...Oh. *Oh.* I forgot these were even here. There used
> to be four of everything, you know. Four altars. Four kinds of work. Four little shrines the old
> monkeys would light when the seasons turned." *(quietly)* "Grandma Mimi used to bring me here. I
> haven't thought about that in… a long time."

Four altars. Four directions. Four corners of the same one island.

---

## ☯ The pattern — `4`, the Four Images

Take the Two Modes and let **each one split again** — yáng can be *young* or *old*, yīn can be *young*
or *old* — and you get the **Sìxiàng (四象), the Four Images**: the four ways two lines can stack.
This is the second doubling: **2 → 4**, which is **2² = 4**, the first *square*.

```
                    ☯ Tàijí
                   ╱        ╲
                ⚊ Yáng      ⚋ Yīn
               ╱    ╲       ╱    ╲
            ⚌      ⚍      ⚎      ⚏
         old yáng young  young  old yīn
                  yīn    yáng
              the FOUR IMAGES        2² = 4
```

The [`yijingjs`](https://github.com/jklarenbeek/yijingjs) engine reads every hexagram as **three
stacked Images** — `yijing_red()`, `yijing_white()`, `yijing_blue()` pull the three pairs of lines.
And [`sixiang.json`](https://github.com/jklarenbeek/yijingjs/blob/master/packages/core/src/sixiang.json)
gives the Four their faces as **directions and elements** — the exact compass at the crossroads:

| Direction | Image | Element (`sixiang.json`) | The grove's corner |
|-----------|:-----:|--------------------------|--------------------|
| **North** | ⚏ old yīn | **Aarde** (Earth) | 🥥 the Sharing Stump |
| **East** | ⚎ young yáng | **Water** | 🌊 the Tide Pools *(already lit!)* |
| **West** | ⚍ young yīn | **Vuur** (Fire) | 🍇 the Vine Heights |
| **South** | ⚌ old yáng | **Lucht** (Air) | 🌱 the Banana Garden |

This is the chapter where **the map opens**. The four worlds of the hub aren't four separate games —
they're the **Four Images of the same One light**, the four directions a single grove faces. Two had
two faces each, and now the whole island is laid out in front of you.

## 🧮 The mathematics — doubling, halving, and the four kinds of work

The number of this chapter is **4 = 2 × 2 = 2²**, so the math is the first taste of **scaling**:

- **Doubling and halving.** The Four Images literally *are* the Two Modes doubled. Mechanically, the
  bridge skill from `+` to `×`: `add → double → double again`. The child meets the idea that you can
  *grow a number by repeating a step* — the seed of multiplication — and *shrink it by undoing the
  step* — the seed of division. (Research: build `×`/`÷` **from** repeated `+`/`−`, with the visual
  model leading — `docs/01-learn.md`.)
- **Place value, gently.** Ten ones become *one ten*: grouping is itself a doubling-style move
  (a smaller unit folded into a bigger one). The Four-Image crossroads is where "a number has parts"
  becomes visible.
- **The four operations, named at last.** `+` and `−` you met as the tide. Now their three siblings
  get their shrines: **×** (Garden / South / Air), **÷** (Stump / North / Earth), **fractions**
  (Vines / West / Fire). The child sees the *whole* curriculum as one compass, not a corridor.

> [!NOTE]
> **Squaring shows up as a shape, not a rule.** `2² = 4` is *two rows of two* — the first array, the
> first little square of bananas. We don't say "squared." We let the child *see* a 2×2 patch and feel
> that "doubling, twice" makes a square. This pre-loads the array mechanic of
> [Chapter 03](CHAPTER_03.md).

## 🌀 The Gray Echo Realm — the Lens of Echoes

This chapter delivers the user's **Zelda Pillar #4 (Dual Worlds)** as a real mechanic, and it falls
straight out of the Four Images: every Image has a **light face and a dark face** (young/old, the two
ends of each mode). Flipping between them is `yijing_invert()` at the scale of a whole room.

- **Sacred tool — the Lens of Echoes.** Found at the crossroads. Look through it and the colorful
  chamber flips into its **Gray Echo Realm** — the same room, drained to gray, where the *scaffolds*
  live: ghost-coconuts you can count, glowing grid lines for arrays, tick-marks on the number-line
  vine.
- **How it plays:** an abstract puzzle in the bright world (a bare equation, an empty number line)
  becomes *concrete* in the Echo Realm. You step into the gray, **draw the model** (line up the
  ghost-coconuts, trace the grid), then **flip back** with the Lens to claim the answer stone in
  color.
- **Why it matters:** the child decides *when they need the picture.* Help isn't pushed on them and
  isn't withheld — it's a place they can choose to step into. Concrete ⇄ abstract becomes a thing you
  *do with your hands*, which is exactly the bridge the research asks for.

The Echo Realm is also the gray island's secret gift: the Crab King drained the color, but the gray
he left behind is *where the scaffolding shows.* The thing that looks like loss is, looked at through
the right Lens, the thing that helps you learn.

## 🌀 Heritage threads

- **Zelda — Pillar #4 (Dual Worlds & Time):** A Link to the Past's Light/Dark World, realized as the
  **Gray Echo Realm**. Actions in the gray (drawing the model) change the bright world (the answer
  appears). The contrast is the puzzle.
- **Uşas (1987):** Konami's MSX gem split its goddess's jewel into **four pieces** and ran on **four
  moods** (Joy / Anger / Sadness / Ease) — a Four-fold game to its bones, with a **hub of doors**.
  Our four altars *are* those four pieces; our Four Images *are* those four moods; the crossroads
  *is* the hub of doors. We borrow the architecture and leave the cruelty for [Chapter 06](CHAPTER_06.md)
  to re-write.

## ✍️ The line is drawn — **line 2, Yīn `⚋`**

Light all four altars (each blooms with its world's color) and the Tide Stone presses the **second**
line — and this one is **yīn**, the receptive line, because the Four Images are the *receiving* of the
two modes into four directions. The grove doesn't just push light out; it makes **room** for it, in
four corners.

```
   ⚋   line 6
   ⚋   line 5
   ⚋   line 4
   ⚋   line 3
   ⚋   line 2   ← NEW. Yīn. The four directions open and receive.
   ⚊   line 1
```

Lines 1–2 now read `⚊` over `⚋` — the bottom two thirds of the **lower trigram, Lí ☲ (Fire)**. The
fire is catching. One more line and the light-half of the island is lit.

> **Mimi:** "Four altars, four ways home. Grandma always said the grove can't be lit from one corner
> — you have to walk all the way around." *(she looks south, toward the Garden, where something is
> already turning green)* "...Speaking of which. You hear that? The bananas are waking up."

## 🛡️ Anti-anxiety & pedagogy notes

- **The Echo Realm is opt-in help, never a penalty.** Stepping into the gray to see the scaffold is a
  *power*, framed like equipping the Lens — the opposite of "you needed the baby version."
- **Reduced-motion safe:** the flip is a cross-fade, not a spin (`a11y.js`); the Echo Realm respects
  high-contrast and colorblind palettes (the scaffolds are shapes, not just colors).
- **The compass shows the whole journey at once.** Seeing all four worlds up front (even locked)
  lowers anxiety — there are no nasty surprises, only corners you haven't walked to yet
  (`island.js`: choices sequence, never exclude).
