**King's Valley II: The Seal of El Giza** (Japanese: 王家の谷 エルギーザの封印 / *Ōke no Tani: El Giza no Fūin*) is a 1988 puzzle-platformer developed and published by Konami for the MSX platform (both MSX1 and MSX2 versions). It is the sequel to the 1985 game *King's Valley*. The game uses a 1-megabit SCC megaROM cartridge (RC760 for MSX1; RC761 for MSX2) and features enhanced sound via the SCC chip.

The MSX2 version offers improved graphics, recolored items and backgrounds, remixed music, a slightly larger screen, and on-screen information (score, lives, etc.) that requires pressing F2 in the MSX1 version. A rare “contest” gold cartridge version exists containing 20 custom stages created by winners of a level-design contest run by Japanese MSX magazines.

Composers include **Kinuyo Yamashita** (alongside Kazuhiko Uehara, Masahiro Ikariko, and others). The game is widely praised as one of the best puzzle-platformers on MSX—deeper and more engaging than similar titles like the *Eggerland* (Lolo) series—thanks to its clever puzzles, strategic tool use, platforming integration, and built-in level editor.

### Story / Plot
Set in the far future, the Vick family continues its fascination with the mysterious powers of the pyramids. Inter-planetary archaeologist **Vick XIII** investigates ancient relics on planets beyond Earth. While exploring pyramids on the planet Remool, he uncovers an inscription revealing shocking truths:

The royal family of ancient Egypt actually originated from Remool. The pyramids on Earth were advanced devices designed to transport the souls of deceased rulers back to their home planet. The treasures buried with the bodies served as control devices for this soul-transportation process.

However, centuries of probing and exploration by archaeologists and scholars (including Vick’s own ancestors) have caused the tombs to malfunction and head toward self-destruction. The accumulated power within the pyramids is now sufficient to destroy Earth entirely.

The only way to save the planet is for Vick XIII to travel to **El Giza**—the central pyramid linked to all others on Earth—and shut down its core functions. To do this, he must navigate through sealed chambers across multiple pyramids, collecting and destroying all the **soul stones** (crystals that act as key energy/control components) along the way. The fate of Earth rests on his success.

The story blends science-fiction, ancient Egyptian mythology, and adventure tropes in a dramatic, high-stakes narrative that justifies the puzzle-solving and exploration.

### Gameplay Mechanics
*King’s Valley II* is a **side-view puzzle-platformer** (fixed/flip-screen perspective) that emphasizes careful planning, resource management, and logical puzzle-solving over pure action. It builds on the original *King’s Valley* but shifts heavily toward puzzles.

Core loop per level:
- Explore tomb-like chambers filled with platforms, walls, blocks, and hazards.
- Use limited tools strategically to break walls, dig paths, or manipulate the environment.
- Collect all **soul stones** (glowing crystals/orbs—some visible and reachable by jumping/walking; most require puzzle-solving to access).
- Solve environmental puzzles (pushing blocks, opening paths, creating routes).
- Evade or eliminate enemies (primarily mummies).
- Reach and unlock the exit door once all required soul stones are collected and puzzles are solved.

**Key Mechanics**:
- **Tools/Equipment** (new or expanded from the predecessor): Drill, hammer, and shovel. These have limited uses or must be used in a precise order—using them incorrectly can permanently block paths or trap the player, forcing a level restart. Tools allow breaking specific walls, digging through certain surfaces, or other interactions.
- **Soul Stones / Crystals**: Primary collectibles. Collecting all of them in a level (and often destroying or using them in context) clears the way to the exit. They have a glowing animation effect in the MSX1 version.
- **Block and Object Manipulation**: Push blocks, break walls strategically, and rearrange the environment to create paths or access hidden areas.
- **Platforming**: Jumping between platforms, navigating vertical spaces, and precise movement are essential alongside puzzles.
- **Enemies**: Mummies haunt the pyramids. They can be avoided through careful movement or destroyed using tools/weapons. Other hazards and traps exist in the tombs.
- **Limited Resources & Risk**: Wrong tool usage order can make a level unsolvable without restarting. This creates tense, thoughtful gameplay where planning ahead is crucial.
- **Progression**: Complete levels to advance through pyramids. Passwords are given after finishing each full pyramid (group of 10 levels), allowing continuation without replaying everything.
- **Special Stages**:
  - **Puzzle Stages**: Hidden in specific levels (e.g., stages 20, 35, 43, 52, 55). Entering them (via a hidden pyramid/portal that appears after jumping on certain spots) rewards many extra lives (around 35, depending on moves used).
  - **Music Stages**: Hidden in other locations (e.g., stages 7, 32, 40, 50, 53). These feature a keyboard interface where you can play the game’s music tracks.
- **In-Game Map**: Press F1 to pause, then F2 to view a map of the current stage (very useful for navigation and planning).
- **Other Controls**: F4 toggles music (game often runs faster without it); F5 restarts the current stage (costs one life).

The gameplay rewards observation, experimentation, and logical thinking. Many soul stones require multi-step solutions involving tools, blocks, and enemy positioning.

### Levels, Structure, and Maps
The game contains **over 60 main levels**, organized into **6 pyramids**, each with its own distinct visual theme (unique wall engravings and color patterns).

- Each pyramid consists of **10 levels**.
- Levels are compact puzzle chambers rather than large open areas—self-contained rooms or small multi-screen sections with platforms, walls, blocks, soul stones, enemies, and tool interaction points.
- Progression feels like exploring deeper into ancient tombs, with increasing puzzle complexity.
- **Passwords**: Awarded after completing each pyramid. These allow jumping ahead. Community tools and BASIC programs exist to generate or calculate passwords for specific stages or lives.
- **Maps**: An in-game map (F2) shows the current stage layout, including platforms, items, and paths. No large official world map exists beyond this, as levels are discrete puzzle rooms. Fan resources and walkthrough videos (available on YouTube) provide visual guidance for specific stages. Custom stages created by players add effectively unlimited content.
- **Bonus/Hidden Content**: Specific stages contain entrances to extra puzzle stages (big life rewards) or music stages. These are accessed by triggering hidden pyramids that turn into portals.
- **Level Editor**: One of the game’s standout features. Players can create, edit, save, and share their own custom stages (saved as .elg files, interchangeable between MSX1 and MSX2 versions). This was a major draw in 1988 and remains popular with enthusiasts. A rare gold contest cartridge included 20 fan-made stages.

Levels emphasize variety in puzzle design—some focus on tool sequencing, others on block pushing, enemy manipulation, or precise platforming to reach soul stones. Difficulty ramps up across the pyramids.

### Items, Tools, and Enemies
- **Soul Stones / Orbs / Crystals**: Main collectibles. Some are out in the open; most are hidden behind breakable walls, require tool use, or involve multi-step puzzles.
- **Tools**: Drill, hammer, and shovel—essential for breaking walls, digging, and environmental interaction. Strategic and limited use is key.
- **Blocks and Movable Objects**: Pushed or manipulated to create paths or block enemies.
- **Treasures**: Thematic collectibles tied to the story (control devices for soul transport).
- **Enemies**: Primarily mummies (patrol or guard areas; can be avoided or eliminated with tools). Other tomb hazards and traps exist.
- **Fruits or Minor Items**: Possibly minor scoring or bonus elements (consistent with era’s platformers).

### Controls and Interface
Standard platformer controls (movement, jump) plus dedicated actions for using tools and interacting with objects. The interface is clean, with MSX2 providing constant on-screen status information. Music can be toggled for performance or preference.

### Technical and Version Differences
- **MSX1 (RC760)**: Core experience; soul stones have glowing animation.
- **MSX2 (RC761)**: Enhanced graphics, recoloring, remixed music, larger visible play area, persistent status display. Minor gameplay parity but visually and aurally superior.
- **Editor & Sharing**: Custom levels work across versions.
- **Sound**: SCC-enhanced music with multiple composers. Toggleable; music stages let players perform tracks.

### Reception, Legacy, and Additional Notes
The game received strong praise (critics around 88%, high player scores) for its deep puzzles, satisfying tool-based mechanics, charming presentation, and innovative level editor. It is often cited as superior to similar puzzle games of the era and remains a highlight of the late MSX library.

**Legacy**:
- Influenced or was referenced in later puzzle-platformers.
- A 2006 commercial game (*Pharaoh’s Curse*) was noted as nearly identical in design (sometimes called a ripoff).
- Active fan community shares custom stages, passwords, and walkthroughs.
- Full playthroughs and longplays are available on YouTube.
- Cheats, secrets, and stage passwords documented on sites like GameFAQs (dozens of codes).

**Tips from Community**:
- Plan tool usage carefully—order matters.
- Use the in-game map frequently.
- Learn enemy (mummy) patterns.
- Explore thoroughly for hidden portals to bonus stages.
- Experiment in the editor to understand mechanics deeply.

*King’s Valley II* stands out as a sophisticated, replayable puzzle-platformer with strong thematic cohesion, excellent production values for MSX, and lasting appeal thanks to its editor and challenging but fair design. It perfectly captures the adventurous spirit of exploring dangerous ancient tombs while delivering brain-teasing gameplay.
